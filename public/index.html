Subjective HES Image Evaluation

This evaluation tool aims to assess the subjective quality of different reconstructed HES images obtained by two models. Three types of tissue are considered: liver (from *** hospital) as well as breast and lung from the public WSSB dataset. 14 samples are used in each organ (and for each model), resulting in a subjective dataset of size 84 images.

Pathologists were asked to provide one quality score among four levels that correspond to poor, medium, high, and very high quality. The score is provided based on the overall HES reconstruction (given the input HE stained image, and its corresponding HES ground truth in case of liver samples), while taking into account the intensity of the generated saffron stain and its distribution throughout the generated image.

Focus on the quality of the saffron staining (intensity and colored tissue regions) added relative to the HE input image, compared to the ground truth in case it is available.

Quality levels: Very High quality, High quality, Medium quality, Poor quality.

Poor quality: absence of saffron in the generated HES image, or saffron presence is not realistic.

Medium quality: saffron is present, but it looks slightly realistic.

High quality: saffron is present and looks realistic.

Very High quality: saffron is present and looks much more realistic

Responses are auto-saved in your browser on this computer.
